# Instructions

Everything you need to know about building a tensioner of your own is in this document. Please make sure you at least skim through each section before proceeding.

## Planning

### How Will It Fit?

Every rig is a little different in design and construction. Whether your rig is suitable for this tensioner depends ona  few factors:
- [x] Do you have a 20XX extrusion or 2" tube behind the seat (at least 0.5M away from the seat's belt holes)?
- [x] Is it strong enough to cope with ~20Kg of upwards pull?
- [x] Will the seat cope with ~20Kg of downward pull (in addition to your own weight)?

If the answer is _yes_ to the above, then you should be able to fit this tensioner.

Most aluminium profile rigs will have te required mounting points for the printable `Extrusion Braket`. If you have a tubular steel rig frame with 2" (50MM) tubing, the `Tubular Bracket` will work; but it must be paired with a truss clamp, to be purchased separately.

Other rig designs may require custom brackets to be designed. A `Bracket Template` model is included in the repository so you can design your own without having to model the complicated motor fitting.

### Motors & Electronics

You should be able to order the Wavehsare [motors](https://www.waveshare.com/wiki/DDSM115) and [control board](http://www.waveshare.com/wiki/DDSM_Driver_HAT_(A)) from the same supplier, as they are almost always stocked together. ## Assembly

### Motors

Since these motors are technically _wheels_, they come pre-fitted with rubber treads. We'll be removing these:
1. Unscrew the three M2.5 bolts holding on motor face plate (using the hex wrench included in the screws kit)
2. Apply pulling force towards the front (away from the axle) and gently wiggle off the rubber tread
3. Keep the rubber treads in case you need to return a motor
